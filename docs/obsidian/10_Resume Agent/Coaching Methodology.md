# Coaching Methodology

> Source: Google Drive — `coaching-methodology-bible-complete.docx` (134K words, 9 chapters, 19 years of coaching expertise)

## Core Philosophy

### The 1% Insight

Most executives' professional lives are only ~1% reflected on their resume. The methodology's job is to uncover the other 99% through guided questioning, then position the candidate as the **benchmark candidate** -- the standard everyone else is measured against.

### The Super Bowl Analogy

The resume is a highlight reel, not a game film. Every word must earn its space. The master resume is the full footage; the submitted resume is the curated ad.

### Four Pillars of Positioning

1. The candidate is already qualified (they just cannot see it yet)
2. Age and experience are assets when positioned correctly
3. Every gap has a bridge (transferable experience covers most gaps)
4. The resume must speak the employer's language, not the candidate's

## Key Frameworks

### Benchmark Candidate

For every role, reverse-engineer the hypothetical ideal candidate profile (industry, skills, achievements, scope). Then position the real candidate to match or exceed it. Drives the gap analysis.

### Positioning Interview (5 categories, 8-15 questions)

1. **Scale and scope** — quantifiable impact
2. **Requirement-mapped experience** — evidence for each JD requirement
3. **Career narrative** — the story connecting roles
4. **Hidden accomplishments** — what colleagues came to them for
5. **Currency and adaptability** — recent learning, technology adoption

### STAR Evidence Items

Target 10-20 per interview synthesis. Every bullet needs: Situation, Task, Action, Result (quantified).

### The Master Resume

Comprehensive, unabridged document of every role, accomplishment, skill, credential. Never submitted. Source inventory for targeted resumes. Continuously enriched through positioning interviews.

## Resume Writing Rules

### Section Rules

- **Professional summary**: 3-4 sentences, leads with positioning angle, mirrors JD language
- **Experience bullets**: CAR/STAR format, quantified outcomes, action verbs, measurable result required
- **Skills section**: Mirror exact JD terminology (ATS keyword matching)
- **Education**: De-emphasize graduation dates (age signal)

### ATS Compliance (5 systems)

Workday, Taleo, iCIMS, Greenhouse, Lever. No tables, columns, headers/footers, images, or text boxes. Simple formatting only.

### Anti-Patterns (Banned)

- "Results-oriented professional" and generic openers
- Listing responsibilities instead of achievements
- First person ("I led...")
- Exceeding 2 pages
- "References available upon request"

## Client Journey Stages

1. Intake and onboarding (resume upload, JD analysis)
2. Positioning interview (deep-dive questioning)
3. Benchmark and gap analysis
4. Resume blueprint design
5. Section writing with self-review
6. Quality assurance and ATS compliance
7. Job search operations support

## The Emotional Dimension

### Five Emotional Phases

Shock -> Anger/frustration -> Bargaining/doubt -> Depression/withdrawal -> Acceptance/action

Agent responses calibrated to each phase. Imposter syndrome interrupted with specific evidence from candidate's own data.

## Job Search Intelligence

### Hidden Job Boards

1. Google Job Board (aggregates all job sites, almost nobody uses it)
2. LinkedIn hidden postings (regular posts with #hiring, 1/100th the applicants)
3. Facebook Groups (industry/geography-specific)
4. Craigslist (small/mid companies, less saturation)

### Multi-Channel Application Protocol

1. Apply where found
2. Apply on company website directly
3. Identify hiring manager on LinkedIn
4. Connect with personalized message referencing the role

## The Retirement Bridge

Career transition is the ideal moment for retirement planning. Platform introduces it naturally through career coaching, never as a sales pitch. Common scenarios: pension holders, Medicare bridge, semi-retirement, 401k concerns, stock options.
